Featured JWST Research

K2-18b: Carbon Dioxide and Methane Confirmed — Possible Hycean World

The James Webb Space Telescope has confirmed the presence of carbon dioxide and methane in the atmosphere of K2-18b, a sub-Neptune exoplanet 124 light-years away in the Leo constellation. Scientists at Cambridge University propose this could indicate a "Hycean world" — a planet with a hydrogen-rich atmosphere and a vast liquid water ocean beneath. The discovery marks the first time potential biosignature-adjacent molecules have been detected on a planet orbiting in the habitable zone of its star.

September 2023 · Updated 2025 · NASA / ESA / Cambridge · 124 ly · Leo constellation
Discovery TESS

TOI-700e: Second Earth-Sized Planet Confirmed in Habitable Zone

TESS confirms a second Earth-sized rocky planet in the habitable zone of TOI-700, a cool M-dwarf star 101 light-years away in the Dorado constellation. TOI-700e orbits its star every 28 days and joins sibling TOI-700d as a rare multi-planet habitable-zone system.

Jan 2023 · TESS Mission ESI 0.82
🪐
Research

TRAPPIST-1: Rocky Planets Confirmed Bone-Dry or Water-Rich

New JWST thermal emission data shows TRAPPIST-1b and 1c likely have no thick atmosphere, while inner planets may harbor significant water beneath rocky crusts. Observations of the outer planets 1e, 1f, and 1g continue to show promise for habitability.

March 2024 · JWST / Spitzer
🌍
Research

Kepler-442b: ML Models Push Habitability Score to 91/100

Updated machine learning habitability models incorporating new spectroscopic data place Kepler-442b among the top five most Earth-like worlds known. The planet orbits a K-type star 1,200 ly away with an orbital period of 112.3 days.

Nov 2024 · Kepler Archive / AI
💧
JWST

LHS 1140b: Water Vapor Hints at Ocean-Bearing Super-Earth

JWST transmission spectroscopy of LHS 1140b, a super-Earth 49 light-years away, reveals a tentative water vapor signal in its atmosphere. If confirmed, this would make LHS 1140b one of the most compelling ocean-world candidates in the nearby stellar neighborhood.

July 2024 · JWST / STScI
🌑
Research

Proxima Centauri b: Tidal Locking Analysis Revisited

New atmospheric circulation models suggest that even a tidally-locked Proxima Centauri b could sustain a stable climate if it possesses a thick enough atmosphere. Temperatures on the day-side terminator could allow liquid water, reviving interest in the nearest exoplanet.

Dec 2024 · ESO / Nature
Discovery

GJ 667Cc: Updated Orbital Parameters Improve HZ Confidence

Precision radial velocity measurements refine the orbit of GJ 667Cc, a super-Earth in the habitable zone of a triple-star system 23 light-years away. The new data confirms the planet spends over 90% of its orbit within the conservative habitable zone.

Oct 2024 · HARPS Spectrograph
JWST Mission

55 Cancri e: Magma Ocean and Shifting Atmosphere Confirmed

JWST's MIRI instrument captures unprecedented thermal maps of 55 Cancri e revealing a dynamic lava world with a volatile atmosphere that appears and disappears as the planet orbits its star every 17.7 hours. The discovery offers insight into early Earth-like geological processes.

May 2024 · MIRI / JWST
Discovery

Wolf 1061c: One of the Closest Potentially Habitable Worlds

At just 13.8 light-years away, Wolf 1061c remains one of the closest known super-Earths in the habitable zone. New high-resolution imaging constraints rule out a large moon companion and refine the planet's bulk composition to a likely rocky interior with possible surface water.

Feb 2025 · AAT / UCSD
